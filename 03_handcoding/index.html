History

Early Origins

  • Ancient Egypt - approx. 1300 BC:The first recorded use of a lathe, a fundamental machining tool, dates back to this period. Early lathes were simple and required two people to operate.
  • Ancient Greece:The Greeks also used lathes, with one person turning the wooden workpiece while the other shaped it with a sharp tool.

Middle Ages

  • Vikings and Medieval Europeans:Bowl turning and pole lathes were utilized during this time. By the Middle Ages, lathes operated with the use of a pedal, making them hands-free.

Industrial Revolution

  • 18th and 19th Centuries:The Industrial Revolution brought significant advancements in machining. Samuel Rehe and Eli Terry are credited with inventing milling machines, and Eli Whitney made major improvements to these machines. Milling machines became vital to industrialization, allowing for the production of identical finished objects.

20th Century

  • Machine Age:The late 19th century to the end of World War II saw the rise of machining as a key manufacturing process. Innovations like numerical control (NC) and later computer numerical control (CNC) revolutionized machining by automating the control of machining tools.

Modern Machining

  • 21st Century:Today, machining is a highly advanced field incorporating technologies like CNC, robotics, and additive manufacturing. Modern machining centers are capable of performing multiple operations with high precision and speed.
