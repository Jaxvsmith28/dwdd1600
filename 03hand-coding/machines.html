Types of Machines

Vertical Mill

Mills use rotating cutting tools to remove material from a stationary workpiece.
They are typically used for machining slots, pockets, holes, flat or contoured surfaces, and 3D shapes.

Lathes

Lathes rotate the workpiece while stationary cutting tools remove material.
They are typically used for producing cylindrical parts like shafts, bushings, threads, and precision diameters.

CNC 5-Axis Machines

Similar to a mill; they move cutting tools (or the part itself) along five axes simultaneously â€” X, Y, Z (linear) plus A and B (rotary axes).
They are typically used complex 3D shapes, undercuts, and precision parts that would require multiple setups on a 3-axis machine.

CNC EDM (Electrical Discharge Machines)

EDMs use electrical sparks to erode material, ideal for very hard metals and intricate cavities that require very high tolerances.
They are typically used for making dies, molds, fine features, and internal geometries impossible to mill.
